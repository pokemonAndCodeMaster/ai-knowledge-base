# Conductors to Orchestrators

## Overview
Addy Osmani explores the evolution of the software engineer from an implementer (coder) to a manager of agents. The transition is categorized into two distinct modes of operation: **Conductor** and **Orchestrator**.

## The Two Modes

### Conductor (Micro-Level)
- **Nature**: Interactive, synchronous, single-agent.
- **Role**: Software engineer guides a single agent (like a soloist) through specific tasks.
- **Tools**: Claude Code (CLI), Gemini CLI, Cursor (interactive chat), Cline.
- **Focus**: Fine-grained steering and real-time iteration.

### Orchestrator (Macro-Level)
- **Nature**: Asynchronous, parallel, multi-agent.
- **Role**: Overseeing a "symphony" of agents working concurrently on different parts of a project.
- **Tools**: GitHub Copilot Agent, Google Jules, OpenAI Codex Agent, Conductor.build.
- **Focus**: Delegation, coordination, and high-level quality control.

## Key Insights
- **Asynchronous Delegation**: Moving AI assistance from the foreground to the background.
- **Artifact Traceability**: Orchestration produces Git-level persistent artifacts (PRs, commits), bringing AI into standard team workflows.
- **Human Role Shift**: Effort is front-loaded (specs) and back-loaded (review), freeing up "the middle" for design and strategy.
