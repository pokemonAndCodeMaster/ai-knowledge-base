Rather than being loaded with features that exist just for the sake of AI, it offers capabilities that an average user will actually reach for after trying it once. But here's a little secret — if you're only using NotebookLM as a glorified PDF reader, you're barely scratching the surface of what it can do.\n\n✕ Remove Ads\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/premium/?ref=ad_removal\n\nThe real power lies in how you prompt it. Here are 6 smart prompts to help you get the most out of NotebookLM and its Studio outputs.\n\nI've been using all of these prompts for a while now. Some I picked up from forums like Reddit or X, and others I've created and fine-tuned myself over time.\n\nPresentation Slide Template\n\nGet presentation-ready decks\n\nClose\n\nA few months back, Google added a feature called Slide Deck to NotebookLM, which lets you \n\nconvert sources uploaded to a notebook into a full-fledged presentation\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/notebooklm-turns-my-notes-into-diagrams-instantly/\n\n.\n\nThis feature is powered by \n\nGoogle's Nano Banana image model\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/turn-recipes-into-infographics-with-nano-banana/\n\n, and you can currently create two Slide Deck formats: \n\nDetailed Deck\n\n or \n\nPresenter Slides\n\n. The Detailed Deck is perfect for generating a comprehensive presentation with maximum information from your sources, while Presenter Slides are optimized for on-screen clarity, focusing on concise bullet points and visuals that make your talk easy to follow.\n\nIn addition to selecting the format and language of the Slide Deck, you can also choose between Short or Default deck lengths. However, if you hit the Generate button after configuring these settings, the deck produced might feel very generic.\n\nWhile it will be customized to your sources and include only relevant visuals that reflect the content in your notebook, you can really only use it as a starting point. And since NotebookLM doesn't let you edit Slide Decks just yet, the output can't serve as more than a rough draft or baseline.\n\n✕ Remove Ads\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/premium/?ref=ad_removal\n\nThat said, I've found that a detailed prompt works wonders with the Slide Deck feature. By explicitly specifying everything, from the presentation title and what each slide should include to the overall design scheme and visual style, you can push NotebookLM to generate a deck that feels far more intentional and presentation-ready right out of the gate.\n\nHere's a prompt you can tweak that I rely on extensively:I have attached a script for a [X-minute] presentation delivered to [audience type] titled \"[Presentation Title].\"\n\nPlease generate a Slide-by-Slide Outline for this script. For each slide, provide:\n\nSlide Title: Catchy and relevant to the target audience.\n\nVisual Description: Specific instructions on what image, diagram, or screenshot should appear on the slide. (Include placeholders for any interactive elements, like QR codes, if needed.)\n\nOn-Screen Text: Maximum 3–5 bullet points. Keep it minimal. Please not paste the whole script. Use keywords or technical terms relevant to the topic.\n\nDesign Style: [e.g., Dark mode, minimalist, professional, playful, futuristic — choose a style that fits your audience].\n\nOptional Guidance on Key Sections: Provide your own headings if you like, but consider including:\n\nIntroduction / Hook\n\nProblem / Challenge\n\nSolution / Approach\n\nInteractive Moment / Example\n\nCore Concept / Key Terms\n\nConclusion / Takeaways\n\nThe \"five essential questions\" prompt\n\nStop using NotebookLM for just answers\n\n✕ Remove Ads\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/premium/?ref=ad_removal\n\nNotebookLM's Chat functionality is a great way to interrogate your sources and surface what actually matters. A lot of people use NotebookLM solely to cross-question their sources. However, there are only so many individual questions you can ask before things start to feel fragmented or repetitive. Fortunately, there's one prompt that I found on Reddit that flips this process around.\n\nInstead of you doing all the probing, it asks NotebookLM to step back, identify the core ideas across everything you've uploaded, and frame them as five essential questions. While I prefer personalizing the prompt based on what my notebook is about, here's the generic version:\n\n1.) Analyze all inputs and generate 5 essential questions that, when answered, capture the main points and core meaning of all inputs.\n\n2.) When formulating your questions:\n\na. Address the central theme (or themes if there are many) or argument (or arguments if many).\n\nb. Identify key supporting ideas\n\nc. Highlight important facts or evidence\n\nd. Reveal the author's purpose or perspective\n\ne. Explore any significant implications or conclusions.\n\n3.) Answer all of your generated questions one-by-one in detail.\n\nAudio Overview formats\n\nA mixed bag\n\nTashreef Shareef / MakeUseOf Credit: Tashreef Shareef / MakeUseOf\n\n✕ Remove Ads\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/premium/?ref=ad_removal\n\nNotebookLM's viral Audio Overview feature, which lets you convert your sources into engaging podcasts, is one of my favorite ways to passively learn. I generate a podcast, plug in my AirPods, and learn on the go. With Audio Overviews, I've noticed that the more you generate and listen to them, the more monotonous they can become.\n\n \n\nRelated\n\nIf you like NotebookLM, you're going to love this new Google tool\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/illuminate-google-labs-experiment/\n\nTake my word for it.\n\nPosts \n\n6\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/illuminate-google-labs-experiment/#threads\n\nBy \n\nMahnoor Faisal\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/author/mahnoor-faisal/\n\nDec 27, 2025\n\nEven if they're about wildly different topics, you'll begin noticing patterns in the way the hosts speak, explain ideas, and transition between prompts. That's why customizing them with prompts makes such a big difference. Here are some of my favorite Audio Overview prompts:\n\nThe “Teach It to a 12-Year-Old” prompt\n\nHave you heard about the Feynman Technique? Chances are, you know about the technique — you just might not know it by name. At its core, the Feynman Technique is about learning by teaching: if you can explain a concept simply and clearly, you actually understand it. And if you can't, that's usually a sign there are gaps in your knowledge. Here's a prompt that applies this idea directly to NotebookLM's Audio Overviews:\n\n\"Explain the core concepts in this source as if you are teaching a smart 12-year-old. Use analogies for every complex term. If there is a concept that is abstract, ground it in a real-world physical example. Focus on why this matters, not just what it is.\"\n\nQuick meeting brief\n\nMeeting notes have fundamentally changed since AI entered the game, and NotebookLM's Audio Overviews feature is an excellent way to passively get a recap of key points. However, a conversational, podcast-like discussion isn't always the most efficient way to absorb information when you're pressed for time. That's where the prompt below comes in handy:\n\n✕ Remove Ads\n\nhttps://www.makeuseof.com/premium/?ref=ad_removal\n\n\"You are briefing a busy CEO. Do not use fluff or banter. Go straight to the bottom line: What is the problem, what is the solution, and what are the financial implications? If there are action items in the text, list them clearly. Keep the tone professional, crisp, and urgent.\"\n\nThe “Chaotic Podcast Hosts” Prompt\n\nSometimes, you just want to listen to a really, really chaotic podcast. Why not convert your sources into a wild, improvisational discussion using NotebookLM? With this prompt I found on Reddit (and slightly polished myself), you can generate scenarios where hosts clash, trade witty insults, and riff off improvised backstories. It's an incredibly fun way to listen to your sources in a completely different yet entertaining format.\n\nHost names: Amber & Rick.\n\nHosts dislike each other and the topics they're discussing.\n\nDialogue style: Dry, sly, witty, and hilariously underhanded insults. Incorporate sarcasm, thinly veiled cynicism, and unrestrained contempt.\n\nStorytelling: Improvise implied backstory, history, relationships, and personal knowledge to create fodder for embarrassment or humiliation. Include a few underlying story arcs that gradually unfold.\n\nEscalation: Have the hosts go at each other increasingly viciously until they lose their temper and yell venomously.\n\nResolution: End with one host making a completely unexpected, hilarious joke that breaks the tension and makes both laugh uncontrollably.\n\nThere are endless possibilities\n\nThe six prompts I've covered above are just the tip of the iceberg.
